# References

Some of the ideas behind this movement are based on published peer-reviewed articles and other sources:

- [Angelici, M., & Profeta, P. (2024). Smart working: work flexibility without constraints. _Management Science_, 70(3), 1680-1705.](https://doi.org/10.1287/mnsc.2023.4767), where they found evidence of performance and work-life balance improvement with flexible time/place of work.
- [Ding, Y., & Ma, M. S. (2023). Return-to-office mandates. _Available at SSRN 4675401._](http://dx.doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.4675401), where they conclude that RTO mandates reduce satisfaction of the employees without benefits for the company.
- [Höcker, M. C., Bachtal, Y., Voll, K., & Pfnür, A. (2024). Healthy, healthier, hybrid work: the burnout-reducing potential of remote work and the mediating effect of work autonomy. \_International Journal of Workplace Health d, 17(4), 319-334.](https://doi.org/10.1108/IJWHM-02-2024-0036), where they suggest that employees autonomy in a hybrid-work setup is best for health.
